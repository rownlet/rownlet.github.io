Mapping Two Very Different Global Climate Change Scenarios in the 21st Century
This data visualization project explores the amount of global warming that is projected to occur for each country over the course of the next eighty years. By working with data provided by the World Bank and outputs from some of IPCC's global circulation models, we can forecast what our world may be like under two very different global climate change scenarios, driven mainly by policy action (or inaction) in the 21st century.

Full Production Web App and Book Recommender Engine in Spark
As a final project for one of my graduate school classes on recommender systems, I decided to build a full production book recommendation engine. Utilizing the goodbooks-10k dataset, which contains close to 1 million book ratings, I built an alternating least squares (ALS) model in Spark and Databricks, deployed it to AWS, and built a front-end python Flask application to serve book recommendations to users. The application has a complete user registration/sign-in function, and serves recommendations based on the ALS predictions and a method of user-based collaborative filtering.

Linear Regression and Election Data
A classmate and I created a predictive model that calculates the proportion of votes for Donald Trump in the 2016 election based on responses to a select number of value-based questions from the American Values Survey, administered by the Public Religion Research Institute (PRRI) in 2017. We used R to manipulate our data, run our regression models, and calculate our predictions. I used D3.js to create a map of our model's performance against the actual percentage of voters that voted for Donald Trump in each state.
